Welcome to the World of Sand Stories Meet
Dr. K. Latha Maheswari

Dr. K. Latha Maheswari is an artist who has spent over 25 years turning her love for art into something meaningful. Born and raised in Madurai, she started small in 2001 by opening the VAC Academy of Fine Arts, a space where she taught and inspired thousands of students to follow their creative dreams. Her life changed when she discovered sand art — an art form that lets her tell stories using just her hands and grains of sand. What began as curiosity soon became her identity. Today, her sand stories bring emotions to life, blending light, rhythm, and feeling in every frame.

Dr. Latha’s performances are known to leave audiences silent, calm, and deeply moved. She has performed at weddings, corporate events, and cultural shows across India, each time creating something new and heartfelt. To her, sand art is not just about drawing; it’s about expressing life — simple, beautiful. Every design she creates may fade away, but the emotion it leaves behind stays forever. From her early days in Madurai to performing for A.R. Rahman and being featured in Rajinikanth’s Vettaiyan, her journey is proof that true art always finds its way to people’s hearts.

Her Performances & Recognition

Dr. Latha’s storytelling in sand has been seen in over 600 live performances—private celebrations, corporate launches, televised cultural programmes and more. Her art’s distinct approach was recognised early on: in 2014, she was invited to create live sand animation for the song “Kismet Se” in an album by A. R. Rahman—a turning point that proved her medium and method could stand beside music legends. She has also made her mark on TV and film. One of her notable performances was featured in the starring-film Vettaiyan with Rajinikanth—bringing her sand art into mainstream visual storytelling and exposing wide audiences to the art form.

What sets Dr. Latha apart is how each show is crafted: she doesn’t simply perform; she synchronises music, narration and visuals with fine-grained movement of sand under the lights. As she herself says, “There is absolutely no room for error.” Her recognition extends beyond stage and screen. Media outlets, television talk shows and cultural magazines have highlighted her as “the only woman sand artist in Chennai” at one point a reflection of how rare and distinctive her work is.
